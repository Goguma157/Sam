NY School Immunization Report (STAT 380)

Power BI Data Visualization ARC GIS Maps

Project Overview

This project for STAT 380 involved analyzing the School Immunization Survey data from the NY State Health Department. The objective was to use Power BI to create a series of visualizations and an interactive report to explain the data.

Key Visualizations & Analysis

The analysis focused on the relationship between immunization rates and exemption types across different schools. Below are the key charts and interpretations derived from the data.

Immunization Rate by Exemption Type

A scatter plot was created to analyze the relationship between religious exemptions and measles immunization rates.

Interpretation of Key Finding

From the scatter plot, a strong inverse relationship was observed between the percentage of religious exemptions in private schools and the percentage of students immunized against Measles. This suggests that religious exemptions are a primary factor driving lower Measles immunization rates across these schools. In contrast, the scatterplot using medical exemptions showed no discernible trend.

Final Interactive Report

The individual visualizations were compiled into a final, interactive report page. This dashboard includes the scatter plots, a column chart comparing immunization rates by school type, summary cards for Polio and Varicella rates, and a slicer for the reporting period.

Conclusion

This project demonstrated the ability to take a raw dataset and transform it into a compelling, interactive story using Power BI. The key takeaway was identifying that religious exemptions, not medical ones, were the primary driver of lower immunization rates, a crucial insight made clear through effective data visualization.
