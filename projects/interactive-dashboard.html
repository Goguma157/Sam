CDC 500 Cities: Health Data Visualization (STAT 380)

Power BI Data Visualization ARC GIS Maps

Project Overview

This lab for STAT 380 focused on creating and formatting various visualizations using Power BI. The project utilized the "500 Cities" dataset from the CDC to explore health metrics across the United States. Key tasks included data transformation, creating ARC GIS and bubble maps, and building scatter plots to analyze relationships between different health indicators.

Key Visualizations & Analysis

The analysis involved creating several map types and scatter plots to identify trends in the public health data.

ARC GIS Map of Stroke Prevalence

An ARC GIS map was created to visualize the adjusted prevalence of strokes across different locations in the United States. The size of the bubbles corresponds to the prevalence rate.

Scatter Plot: No Dental Visit vs. Chronic Kidney Diseases

Across all regions, the analysis revealed a clear inverse correlation: populations who frequently visit the dentist showed a significantly lower risk of developing Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). However, it's important to note that a direct causal relationship between the dental checkups themselves and CKD was not established. Instead, this correlation strongly suggests the influence of a lurking variable: general health consciousness. People who visit the dentist regularly tend to be those who prioritize and put more effort into overall health management than those who do not seek regular preventative care. This higher level of health awareness and proactive management is likely contributing positively to the prevention of CKD, as well as other chronic diseases.
